Top 9 Things to Do in Edinburgh

Edinburgh is so diverse - you can go hiking, do pottery, visit bookshops, and even go to the beach all in the same day. Some popular tourist attractions are listed below. Don’t forget to explore the streets of Edinburgh and enter small shops and stores, which can be hidden gems!

click a box to know more!

Arthur’s Seat

Description:

Hike up Arthur's Seat for breathtaking views of the city. It takes around an hour, but it’s worth it! Try the route from Holyrood Park for an easier way up, where you’ll also find swans and lots of greenery.

Location:

Holyrood Park, Edinburgh

Open:

Always open

Dean Village

Description:

Explore the quaint streets of Dean Village, a peaceful hidden gem in Edinburgh.

Location:

Dean Path, Edinburgh

Open:

Always open

Royal Mile

Description:

Walk down the historic Royal Mile, connecting Edinburgh Castle and Holyrood Palace. Don’t miss Victoria Street with its colorful buildings and unique stores!

Location:

Royal Mile, Edinburgh

Open:

Always open

Edinburgh Castle

Description:

Visit the iconic Edinburgh Castle on an extinct volcano. Enjoy city views and learn about Scotland's history. Be sure to visit Ross Fountain and the Princes Street Gardens as well.

Location:

Castlehill, Edinburgh

Open:

Daily, 9:30 am - 5:00 pm

Calton Hill

Description:

Climb Calton Hill for stunning views and to see the National Monument of Scotland. If you're lucky, you might even catch the Northern Lights here on a clear night.

Location:

Calton Hill, Edinburgh

Open:

Always open

National Museum of Scotland

Description:

Explore Scotland's history, art, and science in this museum. From ancient relics to modern displays, there's something for everyone.

Location:

Chambers Street, Edinburgh

Open:

Daily, 10:00 am - 5:00 pm

Camera Obscura

Description:

Step into a world of optical illusions and interactive exhibits. Fun for all ages, this attraction provides a unique perspective on the city.

Location:

Castlehill, Edinburgh

Open:

Daily, 9:00 am - 6:00 pm

Greyfriars Kirkyard

Description:

Wander through this historic cemetery, known for its connections to Harry Potter and Edinburgh’s past. Look out for Greyfriars Bobby’s grave!

Location:

Greyfriars Place, Edinburgh

Open:

Always open

Edinburgh Zoo

Description:

Home to a wide range of animals, including pandas and penguins. The zoo is perfect for family outings and offers daily animal talks and feeding sessions.

Location:

Corstorphine Road, Edinburgh

Open:

Daily, 10:00 am - 5:00 pm
